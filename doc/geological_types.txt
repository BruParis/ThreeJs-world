Keep your geological type as a simple enum value for the tile, fold-and-thrust included. But consider having a small companion struct on tiles that holds optional extra data — an intensity float and maybe an "age" counter. Many of your types would leave these at default. Orogeny, fold-and-thrust, and basin would actually use them. The direction you almost certainly don't need to store since it's always recoverable from boundary geometry and plate motion.
This keeps your enum clean and your core data simple, while letting the types that benefit from nuance carry a bit more information without inflating the whole system.
